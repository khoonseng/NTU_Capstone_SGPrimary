# Types of schools
Source: Types of schools from https://www.moe.gov.sg/education-in-sg/our-schools/types-of-schools
Last checked: 2026-05-29
Applies to: 2026 P1 Registration Exercise

There are different types of schools in Singapore's education system such as Government schools and Government-aided schools. Some Government schools and Government-aided schools are also Special Assistance Plan (SAP) schools that are committed to nurturing bilingual and bicultural students who are immersed in the Chinese language and culture, and are equipped with a global outlook. Some Government schools and Government-aided schools also offer the Gifted Education Programme (GEP) and are committed to ensuring that the potential of each intellectually gifted student is recognised, nurtured and developed.

## Key rules
Government schools offer high quality education such as unique electives, applied subjects and student development programmes at standardised fees. Some government schools have an autonomous status. This means that they follow the national syllabus, but offer a wider range of programmes that enhance your child’s learning experience and develop their talents. Some government schools are also Special Assistance Plan (SAP) schools that are committed to nurturing bilingual and bicultural students who are immersed in the Chinese language and culture, and are equipped with a global outlook.

Government-aided schools are schools set up by various community organisations to cater to the educational needs of their respective communities. They maintain the same education standards as Government schools and charge standardised fees. Some government-aided schools have an autonomous status. This means that they follow the national syllabus, but offer a wider range of programmes that enhance your child’s learning experience and develop their talents. Some government-aided schools are also Special Assistance Plan (SAP) schools that are committed to nurturing bilingual and bicultural students who are immersed in the Chinese language and culture, and are equipped with a global outlook.

The Special Assistance Plan (SAP) was introduced in 1979 to preserve the best traditions of Chinese medium schools while ensuring students are equally proficient in both English and Chinese. The key features of SAP schools include having both English and Chinese as first languages, strong Chinese cultural programmes, emphasis on Chinese values and traditions, overseas immersion to China/Taiwan, and Bicultural Studies Programme (BSP) option. Children applying for SAP primary schools must take Chinese as Mother Tongue Language. Students taking Malay or Tamil as Mother Tongue cannot apply to SAP schools as these SAP schools only offer Chinese as the Mother Tongue language.

From 2027, the Gifted Education Programme (GEP) in its current form will be discontinued and cease admitting new Primary 4 students. Existing classes will continue until 2028 when the last batch completes Primary 6. The GEP is a programme that caters for the needs of intellectually gifted students. Students are identified for the GEP through a 2-stage exercise in Primary 3. Selected students will be invited to join the programme in Primary 4. The GEP curriculum is an enriched curriculum designed to meet the needs of GEP students. The enriched curriculum covers the same content areas as those in the mainstream, but is extended in breadth and depth. GEP students will learn skills for independent inquiry and are encouraged to do explorations on self-selected areas of interest. GEP students have rich opportunities to interact with their schoolmates through school-wide activities, CCAs and Values-in-Action (VIA) programmes, where they learn, work and play together daily. The goals of the GEP are to develop intellectual depth and higher level thinking, nurture productive creativity, develop attitudes for self-directed lifelong learning,enhance aspirations for individual excellence and fulfilment, develop a strong social conscience and commitment to serve society and the nation, and develop moral values and qualities for responsible leadership.
