# Primary 1 (P1) registration - Understand how balloting works
Source: Primary 1 (P1) registration - Understand how balloting works from Primary 1 (P1) registration - Understand how balloting works
Last checked: 2026-05-29
Applies to: 2026 P1 Registration Exercise

Balloting can happen at Phases 2A to 2C Supplementary of the Primary 1 (P1) Registration Exercise. Balloting is conducted when the number of applicants exceeds the school vacancies, which can happen in any phase from 2A to 2C Supplementary. Computerised balloting will be conducted centrally by MOE. All applicants who are required to ballot are assured of an equal chance for admission into the school.


## Key rules
For allocation of places, to ensure continued open access to all schools in later phases, we are reserving 60 places in each school for Phases 2B and 2C at the start of the P1 Registration Exercise, with 20 places for Phase 2B and 40 places for Phase 2C. In addition to the reserved places, At the end of Phase 2A, One-third of any remaining places is allocated to Phase 2B. At the end of Phase 2A, Two-thirds of any remaining places is allocated to Phase 2C. At the end of Phase 2B, any remaining places are allocated to Phase 2C. Balloting can happen at Phases 2A to 2C Supplementary of the Primary 1 (P1) Registration Exercise. Balloting is conducted when the number of applicants exceeds the school vacancies, which can happen in any phase from 2A to 2C Supplementary. Computerised balloting will be conducted centrally by MOE. All applicants who are required to ballot are assured of an equal chance for admission into the school.

Singapore Citizens (SC) have priority over Permanent Residents (PR). Applicants are sorted into 6 priority groups and priority admission is given in this order. Under Group 1 (SC<1), Singapore Citizens (SC) living within 1km of the school will have the first priority. Under Group 2 (SC1-2), Singapore Citizens (SC) living between 1km and 2km of the school will have the second priority. Under Group 3 (SC>2), Singapore Citizens (SC) living outside 2km of the school will have the third priority. Under Group 4 (PR<1), Permanent Residents (PR) living within 1km of the school will have the fourth priority. Under Group 5 (PR1-2), Permanent Residents (PR) living between 1km and 2km of the school will have the fifth priority. Under Group 6 (PR>2), Permanent Residents (PR) living outside 2km of the school will have the sixth priority.

Vacancies are given out in order from Group 1 to Group 6. If a group has more applicants than the remaining vacancies, only that group ballots. Others below are excluded. 

In the first example where there are 80 vacancies and 95 applicants. Group 1 (SC<1) has 60 applicants, Group 2 (SC1-2) has 10 applicants, Group 3 (SC>2) has 5 applicants, Group 4 (PR<1) has 10 applicants, Group 5 (PR1-2) has 10 applicants, and Group 6 (PR>2) has 0 applicants. Since the number of applicants under Groups 1 to 3 is 75 (60 + 10 + 5), and is less than the 80 vacancies, all 75 applicants will get in. The remaining 5 places will go to Group 4 (which has 10 applicants), so balloting occurs for 5 spots. Groups 5 and 6 do not ballot or get a chance, and will be unsuccessful in the registration.

In the second example where there are 70 vacancies and 95 applicants. Group 1 (SC<1) has 60 applicants, Group 2 (SC1-2) has 20 applicants, Group 3 (SC>2) has 5 applicants, Group 4 (PR<1) has 5 applicants, Group 5 (PR1-2) has 5 applicants, and Group 6 (PR>2) has 0 applicants. Since the number of applicants under Group 1 is 60, and is less than the 70 vacancies, all 60 applicants will get in. The remaining 10 places will go to Group 2 (which has 20 applicants), so balloting occurs for 10 spots. Groups 3 to 6 do not ballot or get a chance, and will be unsuccessful in the registration.
