# Primary 1 (P1) registration - Understand how balloting works
Source: Primary 1 (P1) registration - Understand how balloting works from Primary 1 (P1) registration - Understand how balloting works
Last checked: 2026-05-29
Applies to: 2026 P1 Registration Exercise

Balloting can happen at Phases 2A to 2C Supplementary of the Primary 1 (P1) Registration Exercise. Balloting is conducted when the number of applicants exceeds the school vacancies, which can happen in any phase from 2A to 2C Supplementary. Computerised balloting will be conducted centrally by MOE. All applicants who are required to ballot are assured of an equal chance for admission into the school.


## Key rules
For allocation of places, to ensure continued open access to all schools in later phases, we are reserving 60 places in each school for Phases 2B and 2C at the start of the P1 Registration Exercise, with 20 places for Phase 2B and 40 places for Phase 2C. In addition to the reserved places, At the end of Phase 2A, One-third of any remaining places is allocated to Phase 2B. At the end of Phase 2A, Two-thirds of any remaining places is allocated to Phase 2C. At the end of Phase 2B, any remaining places are allocated to Phase 2C. Balloting can happen at Phases 2A to 2C Supplementary of the Primary 1 (P1) Registration Exercise. Balloting is conducted when the number of applicants exceeds the school vacancies, which can happen in any phase from 2A to 2C Supplementary. Computerised balloting will be conducted centrally by MOE. All applicants who are required to ballot are assured of an equal chance for admission into the school.

Singapore Citizens (SC) have priority over Permanent Residents (PR). Priority admission is given in this order. Singapore Citizens (SC) living within 1km of the school will have the first priority. Singapore Citizens (SC) living between 1km and 2km of the school will have the second priority. Singapore Citizens (SC) living outside 2km of the school will have the third priority. Permanent Residents (PR) living within 1km of the school will have the fourth priority. Permanent Residents (PR) living between 1km and 2km of the school will have the fifth priority. Permanent Residents (PR) living outside 2km of the school will have the sixth priority.
