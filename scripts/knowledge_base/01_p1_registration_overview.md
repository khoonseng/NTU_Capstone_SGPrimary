# Primary 1 (P1) registration
Source: Primary 1 (P1) registration from https://www.moe.gov.sg/primary/p1-registration
Last checked: 2026-05-29
Applies to: 2026 P1 Registration Exercise

Primary 1 registration is a process to register your child for mainstream primary schools in the following year. The registration exercise for Singapore Citizens (SC) and Permanent Residents (PR) is conducted online, from June 2026 to August 2026. It takes place over 5 phases: Phase 1, 2A, 2B, 2C and 2C Supplementary. Each phase has its eligibility criteria and registration period. You can register in the phase for which your child is eligible.


## Key rules for Singapore Citizen and Permanent Resident children
P1 registration for Singapore Citizen and Permanent Resident children is conducted online through the P1 Registration Portal, from June to August. You can use your Singpass to register and check registration results through the portal, whether you are in Singapore or overseas. You can register your child in their eligible phases, from the 5 phases: Phase 1, 2A, 2B, 2C and 2C Supplementary. For Singapore Citizen and Permanent Resident children, please register your child for only one school in any phase. Multiple applications are not accepted. MOE will take your latest submission made during the phase. If your application in a phase is unsuccessful, you can register your child in the next phase they are eligible for. If your child is unsuccessful in Phase 2C Supplementary, they will be posted to a school with available vacancy.

If an older sibling is currently on Leave of Absence (LOA) from a primary school, you can register your child in Phase 1 for that school. If an older sibling was previously on Leave of Absence (LOA) and has studied in a primary school, you can register your child in Phase 2A for that school. You may register using your NRIC address, a yet-to-be-completed property address or a resale property address. If your child is allocated a place in a school through the P1 registration process but is unable to return to Singapore to start school in January 2027, you can apply for a Leave of Absence (LOA) from your child's school.

You may register your child in a phase as long as you fulfil at least one of the requirements for that phase. 

## Phase 1
Phase 1 is for a child who has a sibling studying in the primary school. All eligible Phase 1 applicants will be offered a place. There is no balloting. 

## Phase 2A
Phase 2A is for children whose parent or sibling is a former student of the primary school, or whose parent is a member of the School Advisory or Management Committee, or whose parent is a staff member of the primary school, or for children studying in the MOE Kindergarten in the primary school. If there is oversubscription where applications exceed vacancies, balloting will take place.

## Phase 2B
Phase 2B is for children whose parent has joined the primary school as a parent volunteer not later than 1 July of the year before P1 registration and has given at least 40 hours of voluntary service to the school by 30 June of the year of P1 registration, or whose parent is a member endorsed by the church or clan directly connected with the primary school, or whose parent is endorsed as an active grassroots leader. Endorsed active grassroots leaders are eligible to register their child only for schools within 2km of their residential address in Phase 2B. MOE will reserve 20 places in each primary school for Phase 2B to ensure continued open access to all primary schools. If there is oversubscription where applications exceed vacancies, balloting will take place. 

## Phase 2C
Phase 2C is open to all children who have not yet registered in a primary school in earlier phases. MOE will reserve 40 places in each primary school for Phase 2C to ensure continued open access to all primary schools, regardless of whether children have prior connections to the school. If there is oversubscription where applications exceed vacancies, balloting will take place. 

## Phase 2C Supplementary
Phase 2C Supplementary is for children who are not yet registered in a primary school after Phase 2C. If your child is unsuccessful in Phase 2C Supplementary, they will be posted to a school with available vacancy by MOE.

## Registration window
Phase 1 Registration starts from 9am on Tuesday, 30 June 2026 and ends on 4.30pm on Thursday, 2 July 2026. The result will be released on Wednesday, 8 July 2026. All eligible Phase 1 applicants will be offered a place. Phase 2A Registration starts from 9am on Thursday, 9 July 2026 and ends on 4.30pm on Friday, 10 July 2026. The result will be released on Friday, 17 July 2026. Phase 2B Registration starts from 9am on Monday, 20 July 2026 and ends on 4.30pm on Tuesday, 21 July 2026. The result will be released on Monday, 27 July 2026. Phase 2C Registration starts from 9am on Tuesday, 28 July 2026 and ends on 4.30pm on Thursday, 30 July 2026. The result will be released on Tuesday, 11 August 2026. Phase 2C Supplementary Registration starts from 9am on Monday, 17 August 2026 and ends on 4.30pm on Tuesday, 18 August 2026. The result will be released on Thursday, 27 August 2026. If your registration is unsuccessful in Phase 2C Supplementary, your child will be posted to a school with available vacancy.

Parents of Singapore Citizen (SC) and Permanent Resident (PR) children can register through the P1 Registration Portal using their Singpass. On the portal, you can view your child’s eligible registration phases and schools from 9am on Tuesday, 30 June 2026. You can register your child during their eligible phases, from Phase 1 to 2C Supplementary. You can check registration results from 8am on results announcement days. You will also be notified of the results through SMS. For more details, please visit https://www.moe.gov.sg/primary/p1-registration/how-to-register. Please also note that P1 registration for international students is a different process.


## Key rules for International students
International students must register through a 2-step process prior to registration in Phase 3. Primary 1 (P1) registration for international students (IS) is for children who are not Singapore Citizens (SC) or Permanent Residents (PR) seeking admission to a mainstream primary school. Admission is not guaranteed due to limited vacancies, as priority is given to Singapore Citizen (SC) and Permanent Resident (PR) children. The period for international students to indicate interest to register for P1 is strictly between 9am on Tuesday, 19 May 2026 to 4.30pm on Monday, 25 May 2026. Please submit your interest through an online form and upload relevant documents during this period. An indication of interest is not considered registration. It is also not first come, first served. There is no option to choose your preferred school. If you miss the deadline, we will not consider any appeals. International students will be participating in Phase 3 registration in October 2026. We will notify you through email by the end of October 2026 whether your child is offered a place in a primary school. If your child is offered a place, we will provide you with the details in the email to register at the designated school. If your child is not offered a place, please consider alternatives such as private schools or taking part in the Admissions Exercise for International Students (AEIS) for admission to Primary 2. If your child becomes a Singapore Citizen or Permanent Resident during the Primary 1 (P1) Registration Exercise (June 2026 to August 2026), they can participate in the phase they are eligible for. For more details, please visit https://www.moe.gov.sg/primary/p1-registration/international-students
