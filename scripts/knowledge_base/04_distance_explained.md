# Primary 1 (P1) registration - How distance affects priority admission

## Key rules
Priority is given to Singapore Citizens and Permanent Residents who live closer to the preferred school when there are more applicants than vacancies. If your chosen primary school has more applicants than vacancies, priority admission will be given based on your child's citizenship and the Home-School Distance (HSD) category, which is in turn based on the address used for registration. Singapore Citizens (SC) have priority over Permanent Residents (PR). Priority admission is given in this order. Singapore Citizens (SC) living within 1km of the school will have the first priority. Singapore Citizens (SC) living between 1km and 2km of the school will have the second priority. Singapore Citizens (SC) living outside 2km of the school will have the third priority. Permanent Residents (PR) living within 1km of the school will have the fourth priority. Permanent Residents (PR) living between 1km and 2km of the school will have the fifth priority. Permanent Residents (PR) living outside 2km of the school will have the sixth priority. 

Detailed address and stay requirements are not covered in this document. For more details, please visit https://www.moe.gov.sg/primary/p1-registration/distance.
